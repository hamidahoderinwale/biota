Build for Emerging Markets

Biota is a fellowship program for motivated undergraduates and recent grads to work on building startups in emerging markets. It involves an online fellowship component alongside a 3-month residency in Lagos.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is a project? A project can be anything that starts with you and your friends, can scale in an interesting way, and be something that people want. It doesn't necessarily have to involve code, applications, or products, and some of the highest leverage projects work directly with people instead of software.

What if I'm already working on a project? You can bring your project to Biota, but be open to the possibility of nudging it in a different direction, especially at the beginning. Additionally, if you don't have a partner for your project, Biota will do their best to help you find one.

Who is Biota for? Biota is a fellowship program that targets driven undergraduates and recent graduates interested in constructing startups in developing markets. It also caters to those who are on a gap year or desire to work on a meaningful project they own or students who want to work on side projects to gain momentum for their careers. Biota is open to anyone who wishes to work with a community of like-minded project-oriented individuals.

What are they looking for? Biota is an inclusive program that doesn't have any specific requirements regarding majors, interests, or class years. Moreover, previous experience is not necessary to apply for this fellowship program. Biota is looking for individuals who have a passion for making a positive impact on society, critically analyze ways for improvements, and enjoy bringing abstract concepts to life.

Who will I be working on projects with? Team-building will take place before the incubator program in the digital component. We encourage you to apply with a team-member, but we also encourage taking the opportunity to work with someone new.
